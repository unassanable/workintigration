Why I Love Cooking

Cooking is my favorite hobby because it combines creativity with science. I enjoy testing flavor combinations, learning new methods, and sharing meals with friends and family. Every dish teaches me something — about balance, timing, and patience.

Favorite Recipes

1. Spiced Tomato Pasta

A simple, comforting pasta made with roasted tomatoes, garlic, and a pinch of chili. Perfect for weeknights.

2. Lemon Herb Chicken

Marinated in citrus and herbs, then roasted until golden — bright, fragrant, and easy to prepare.

Techniques I Practice

  • Sautéing and deglazing to build flavor.
  • Roasting vegetables to concentrate sweetness.
  • Balancing acid, fat, salt, and heat for a rounded dish.

A Small Cooking Story

One evening I tried a new spice blend and accidentally added too much chili — instead of throwing it out, I balanced it with yogurt and lemon. The mistake turned into an unexpectedly brilliant sauce. That taught me: cooking rewards experimentation and rescue.

Quick Tips & Tools

  • Invest in a good chef's knife.
  • Keep a small spice jar for quick seasoning adjustments.
  • Use a thermometer for perfect roasts.

About Cooking

Cooking blends science and creativity — adjusting heat, timing, and seasoning results in meals that bring people together. Below are curated recipes and practical techniques to try at home.

Recipes to Try

One-pan Harissa Chicken

Roast chicken with vegetables and harissa paste for a flavorful, low-effort meal.

Chickpea & Spinach Curry

A vegetarian curry that's quick, nutritious, and full of spices. Serve with rice or flatbread.

Techniques

  1. Build layers of flavor: sweat onions, toast spices, deglaze the pan.
  2. Use resting time for meats to keep them juicy.
  3. Taste early and often — adjust with acid (vinegar, lemon) and fat (butter, oil).

Shopping & Tools

Staples: olive oil, kosher salt, garlic, onions, canned tomatoes, dried spices.
